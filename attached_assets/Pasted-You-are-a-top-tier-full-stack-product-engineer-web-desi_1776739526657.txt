==================================================
NEW PRODUCT VISION
==================================================

We are evolving LayerStack into a sharper, more expressive public-facing product called **Noted**.

Core idea:

Noted is a human-made music platform where songs evolve in public through community contribution.

But unlike the original LayerStack mental model, which focused primarily on full-length musical layer commits, Noted should support a broader and more powerful model:

### 1. Structure contributions
A song first needs a core skeleton:
- drums
- bass
- rhythm/harmony
- lead or hook
- foundational textures
- song-defining long-form layers

These are more foundational and often longer.

### 2. Accent / micro contributions
Once a song has enough structure, the community can start adding smaller, more playful, more signature-like sonic additions:
- coffee pour
- breath
- clap
- glass tap
- room tone
- tiny vocal stab
- one-shot percussion
- found sound
- texture burst
- micro melody fragment
- spoken phrase
- subtle detail moments

This is where the product gets culturally sticky.

A song should not just be “built.”
It should be **noted** into shape — by people leaving sonic fingerprints.

==================================================
RECOMMENDED PRODUCT EVOLUTION
==================================================

I am not telling you the exact implementation.
I am telling you the direction.

A strong solution likely includes most of the following:

### Song lifecycle / mode
A song should clearly live in some phase such as:
- structure
- accents
- maybe later hybrid / archived

This should influence what rounds can be created and how the UI explains participation.

### More expressive round model
The current “target instrument” model should evolve into something richer.

Rounds should express intent, such as:
- kind: structure or accent
- category: drums, bass, found sound, room texture, one-shot, etc.
- merge behavior: single winner vs multi-merge
- the exact base version the round is pinned against

### Better submission semantics
A full-length bass take and a 2-second coffee-pour sound should not feel identical in the UI or domain language.

The system should reflect that they are both valid contributions, but different kinds.

### Better merge model
Accent rounds may naturally support merging multiple good small contributions into the next release.
You should think about whether the admin flow and schema should support that more naturally.

### Better song storytelling
The public song page should make it obvious:
- what phase the song is in
- what contributions built it
- what notes/accent moments shaped it
- what changed between versions
- what the community is being asked for now

### Better concept sell
If a new user lands on the site, the product should explain itself quickly and feel exciting.
